Physics PhD Student · University of Notre Dame

Raymond Kil

I am a PhD candidate in High Energy Physics experiment at the University of Notre Dame. I work on the CMS experiment at CERN, searching for Higgs decays beyond the Standard Model. My work includes statistical modeling, ML-based classification, rare signal detection in large datasets, distributive computing, and data visualization. I also participate in a big update on the CMS detector according to the high-luminosity upgrade of the LHC. That work involves implementing 160 MHz digital signal processing algorithms using High-Level Synthesis and designing C++/SQL/RPC-based tools for real-time detector configuration. I also enjoy solving and numerically modeling a wide range of puzzles and problems, including mathematics, economics, physics, etc.
